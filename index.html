Three landing page variants for the same product (Faith Essentials by AlMaghrib Institute). Different emotional entry points. Built on the Hormozi framework. Ready for A/B testing.

1. The Experiment

Hypothesis

Different segments of our audience arrive at Faith Essentials through fundamentally different emotional states. A single landing page forces everyone through the same door. By creating three distinct entry points, we can:

  • Match ad messaging to landing page experience (reducing drop-off)
  • Speak directly to the specific pain point that brought them here
  • Test which emotional angle converts best for each traffic source

The Three Angles

Version Angle Target Segment Core Insight
A: "The Grounding" Identity / Crisis 22-35, reconnecting with faith They feel the gap between who they are and who they want to be. They're not looking for information — they're looking for grounding.
B: "The Scholars" Access / Authority 28-45, committed learner They're already studying but frustrated with fragmented YouTube content. They want structure, credentials, and a curriculum.
C: "The Gift" Family / Community 30-50, parent or guardian They're not the end user — they're watching someone they love drift. The purchase is an act of care, not self-improvement.

Why These Three?

These aren't arbitrary personas. They map to the three most common paths to Islamic education platforms:

  1. Personal crisis — "I don't know enough and it's becoming a problem"
  2. Structured learning — "I want to study seriously but YouTube isn't cutting it"
  3. Gift/intervention — "Someone I care about needs this"

Version C is particularly interesting — it's rarely addressed by competitors, but gift subscriptions and family purchases represent a significant segment.

5. Recommended Test Plan

Phase 1: Internal Review (Current)

  • Stakeholder feedback on messaging and tone
  • Legal/compliance review of claims
  • Mobile responsiveness check

Phase 2: A/B Test Setup

  • Traffic split: route ad sets to matching landing pages
  • A ads → Version A, B ads → Version B, C ads → Version C
  • Track: CTR, time on page, scroll depth, conversion rate

Phase 3: Analysis

  • Which angle converts best overall?
  • Which angle converts best per traffic source?
  • Is Version C viable? (gift purchases are often underserved)

6. Key Questions for Review

  1. Does each version feel distinct enough, or do they blur together?
  2. Is the gift angle (Version C) worth pursuing, or should we focus on A and B?
  3. Are any claims or framings problematic?
  4. Which version would you personally respond to?
