A Mother's Story

"Faith Essentials did what my words couldn't."

My daughter came home from her first year of college different.

Not in the ways I expected — the independence, the confidence, the opinions. Those were fine. It was the silence about Islam that worried me.

She'd stopped asking questions. When I'd mention a hadith or share something from a lecture, she'd nod politely. She'd pray sometimes, but the consistency was gone. The curiosity was gone. The fire was gone.

I tried everything a mother tries. I forwarded YouTube lectures. I bought her books. I brought up topics at dinner. I even signed her up for a weekend seminar once — she went, but it didn't stick.

The truth I had to face: my words weren't enough. I'm not a scholar. And at 20 years old, she didn't want a lecture from her mother. She wanted to find her own way back — on her own terms, in her own time.

A friend told me about Faith Essentials. I was skeptical. Another app? She already had Quran apps she never opened. But this was different — it had scholars she'd actually heard of. Omar Suleiman. Yasmin Mogahed. Yasir Qadhi. And it had something I hadn't seen before: a daily practice built in. Not just courses. A morning verse with tafsir. Guided adhkar. A streak counter.

I gifted her a subscription and said nothing. No lecture. No "you should do this." Just a text: "I thought you might like this. No pressure."

Two weeks later, she mentioned something Shaykh Omar Suleiman said in one of his courses. At dinner. Casually. Like it was normal.

A month in, she had a 21-day streak and got genuinely frustrated when she almost forgot one evening. She started listening to the morning adhkar while getting ready for class.

She still doesn't pray five times a day. She still has questions I can't answer. But she's asking them again. She's engaged again. She's on a path again.

I didn't bring her back. I gave her a door. She walked through it.
